Rainfall Warning System

Yellow (Monitor)

Heavy Rain (7.5-15mm/hr). Flooding is possible in low-lying areas. Monitor weather conditions.

Orange (Alert)

Intense Rain (15-30mm/hr). Flooding is threatening. Preparedness measures advised.

Red (Emergency)

Torrential Rain (>30mm/hr). Severe flooding expected. Evacuation protocols active.

Operational Terminology

  • PAR

    Philippine Area of Responsibility. The designated area where PAGASA monitors weather disturbances.

  • Determin-
    istic

    Single-Outcome Forecast. A specific prediction (e.g., "It will rain at 2PM"). Useful for immediate planning but doesn't show uncertainty.

  • Probabil-
    istic

    Likelihood Forecast. Expresses uncertainty (e.g., "70% chance of rain"). Better for risk assessment and contingency planning.

  • LPA / HPA

    Low/High Pressure Area. LPA brings clouds and rain (bad weather). HPA brings fair to clear skies (good weather).

  • ITCZ

    Intertropical Convergence Zone. A belt of low pressure around the equator where winds converge, often causing thunderstorms.

  • Amihan

    Northeast Monsoon. Cool, dry wind (Oct-Mar). Brings cold mornings and light rains to the east.

  • Habagat

    Southwest Monsoon. Warm, moist wind (May-Sep). Associated with heavy rainy seasons and typhoons.
